Estimating landscaping materials accurately saves money and prevents project delays. Unlike building materials, landscaping materials are sold by volume or weight — and the conversion depends on material density. This guide covers how to calculate the right amounts for gravel, mulch, sand, and specialty landscaping materials.

## The Basic Landscaping Formula

For most landscaping materials, the calculation is the same:

```
Volume (cubic yards) = Length (ft) × Width (ft) × Depth (in) ÷ 324
```

The number 324 comes from: 27 (cu ft per cu yd) × 12 (inches per foot) = 324.

### Depth Guidelines by Material

| Material | Typical Depth | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Mulch | 2-3 inches | Deeper than 3 inches prevents water penetration |
| Gravel (path) | 2-3 inches | Compacted |
| Gravel (driveway) | 4-6 inches | Multiple layers recommended |
| River rock | 2-3 inches | Larger stone needs deeper coverage |
| Sand (play) | 6-12 inches | Depth varies by use case |
| Decomposed granite | 2-4 inches | Compacted in lifts |
| Limestone base | 4-8 inches | Compacted in 4-inch lifts |
| Rip rap | 6-18 inches | Depth equals stone diameter |

## Gravel and Crushed Stone

Gravel is sold by the cubic yard or ton. The conversion depends on stone size and type:

| Material | Weight (tons per cu yd) |
|----------|----------------------|
| Pea gravel (3/8") | 1.3-1.4 |
| Crushed stone (3/4") | 1.4-1.5 |
| Crushed stone (1.5") | 1.3-1.4 |
| Limestone (3/4") | 1.4-1.5 |
| Decomposed granite | 1.4-1.5 |

### Driveway Gravel Calculator

A typical driveway uses three layers:
1. **Base layer:** 4-6 inches of large crushed stone (2-3 inch)
2. **Middle layer:** 2-3 inches of medium stone (3/4-1 inch)
3. **Surface layer:** 2 inches of fine gravel or limestone

**Example:** 40×12 ft driveway, 6-inch total depth:
1. Volume = 40 × 12 × 6 ÷ 324 = 8.9 cubic yards
2. Total tons (at 1.4 tons/yard) = 12.5 tons
3. Distribute across layers: base 5 tons, middle 4 tons, surface 3.5 tons

## River Rock and Pebbles

River rock is sold by the ton or cubic yard. Because of the gaps between stones, river rock has a lower density than crushed stone:

- **Small river rock (1-3 inches):** ~1.2 tons per cubic yard
- **Large river cobble (3-6 inches):** ~1.1 tons per cubic yard
- **Pea gravel:** ~1.3 tons per cubic yard

## Mulch

Mulch is one of the few materials sold by volume rather than weight:

- **1 cubic yard of mulch:** Covers approximately 108 sq ft at 3 inches deep
- **Bagged mulch:** Typically 2 cubic feet per bag (14 bags = 1 cubic yard)
- **Loose mulch:** Sold by the cubic yard

### Mulch Depth Recommendations

| Area | Recommended Depth |
|------|-----------------|
| Garden beds | 2-3 inches |
| Around trees | 2-3 inches (keep away from trunk) |
| Playgrounds | 6-12 inches (engineered wood fiber) |
| Pathways | 2-3 inches |

**Pro tip:** Never pile mulch against tree trunks or building foundations. This traps moisture and causes rot. Keep a 3-6 inch gap.

## Sand

Sand is used for masonry, concrete, play areas, and leveling:

| Sand Type | Density (lb/cu ft) | Typical Use |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Concrete sand | 100-110 | Mixing concrete, base material |
| Mason sand | 95-105 | Brick mortar, leveling |
| Play sand | 90-100 | Sandboxes, volleyball courts |
| Fill sand | 100-120 | General fill, drainage |

## French Drain Materials

A French drain requires gravel and pipe. For a standard 18-inch deep, 12-inch wide trench:

### Gravel Volume
```
Gravel (cu yd) = Trench Length (ft) × 1.5 (width ft) × 1.5 (depth ft) ÷ 27
```

The 1.5-ft dimensions account for gravel surrounding the pipe: the trench is wider than the pipe on each side.

### Pipe Sizing
| Drain Length | Recommended Pipe Size |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Under 50 ft | 4-inch perforated |
| 50-100 ft | 4-inch perforated |
| 100-200 ft | 6-inch perforated |

### Additional Materials
- **Filter fabric:** Trench length × fabric width (typically 2-3 feet wider than trench)
- **Pipe sock/filter:** Same length as pipe

## Fire Glass and Decorative Fill

Fire glass is sold by weight rather than volume:

| Pit Size | Glass Depth | Glass Required (lbs) |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| 12-inch round | 2 inches | 5-7 |
| 24-inch round | 2 inches | 15-20 |
| 36-inch round | 2 inches | 30-40 |
| 48-inch round | 2 inches | 55-70 |

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Mixing Up Volume and Weight
Materials like gravel are often sold by the ton, but landscape fabric and mulch by the yard. Always confirm the unit before ordering.

### 2. Not Compacting Confirmation
When you order fill material, expect 20-30% compaction. Your delivered volume will compress once placed. Order 20-30% more for deep fills.

### 3. Forgetting Access
A dump truck carries about 10-12 cubic yards. If your access is narrow, you may need smaller deliveries or to move material by wheelbarrow.

### 4. Ordering Too Little for Large Projects
For large landscape projects, order 10-15% extra to account for uneven grading and touch-ups.
