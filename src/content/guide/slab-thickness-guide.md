Choosing the correct concrete slab thickness is critical to the longevity, safety, and load capacity of your structure. Under-designing a slab leads to premature cracking and settlement under loads, while over-designing wastes money on extra ready-mix volume.

For most residential applications, concrete slabs range between **4 inches** (10 cm) and **6 inches** (15 cm) in thickness. Here is how to determine the correct depth for your specific project.

---

## Slab Thickness Recommendation Table

| Project Type | Recommended Thickness | Soil Base / Gravel | Reinforcement Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Walkways & Sidewalks** | 4 inches | 2–4 inches gravel | Welded wire mesh or fiber |
| **Patios & Play Areas** | 4 inches | 4 inches gravel | Welded wire mesh (6x6 W2.9) |
| **Shed Slabs (Light/Medium)** | 4 inches | 4 inches gravel | #3 Rebar at 18" on-center grid |
| **Residential Driveways (Cars)** | 4 inches | 4–6 inches crushed stone | #3 or #4 Rebar grid |
| **Driveways (SUVs/Heavy Pickups)**| 5 inches | 6 inches crushed stone | #4 Rebar at 16" on-center |
| **RV Pads & Heavy Equipment** | 6 inches | 6 inches compacted road base | #4 Rebar at 12" on-center |
| **Garage Floors (Standard)** | 4 inches | 4 inches gravel + vapor barrier | #3 Rebar at 18" on-center |
| **Garage Floors (Heavy Work)** | 5–6 inches | 6 inches gravel + vapor barrier | #4 Rebar grid |

## 4-Inch Slabs vs. 6-Inch Slabs: What's the Difference?

### The 4-Inch Slab (The Residential Standard)
A 4-inch slab is the standard thickness for almost all non-structural residential concrete projects. It is designed to support pedestrian foot traffic, light lawn equipment, and passenger vehicles. 

*   **Load Limit:** Generally supports up to 40 pounds per square inch (psi) of live load if built on well-compacted soil.
*   **Ready-Mix Math:** One cubic yard of concrete covers exactly **81 square feet** at 4 inches thick (before waste factors).

### The 6-Inch Slab (The Heavy-Duty Standard)
A 6-inch slab is used when the concrete must support heavy point loads, such as large recreational vehicles (RVs), commercial delivery trucks, or heavy workshop machinery. 

*   **Load Limit:** Supports heavy heavy-duty traffic and structural walls.
*   **Ready-Mix Math:** One cubic yard covers exactly **54 square feet** at 6 inches thick.

## Crucial Subgrade & Prep Tips

Regardless of thickness, the strength of the concrete depends heavily on the base beneath it:

1.  **Uniform Compaction:** Loose soil under a slab leads to voids. Always use a mechanical plate compactor on the subgrade and gravel base.
2.  **Gravel Sub-Base:** A 4-inch layer of crushed stone (like #57 gravel) provides a capillary break that prevents groundwater from wicking into the concrete and causing freeze-thaw damage.
3.  **Vapor Barriers:** For indoor garage slabs or sheds housing sensitive tools, install a 10-mil poly vapor barrier over the gravel base to prevent moisture from evaporating upward.
4.  **Waste Buffer:** When ordering concrete, always add a **10% waste factor** to your calculator volume. Excavation limits are rarely perfectly uniform, and formwork flexing can easily consume an extra 5% to 8% of ready-mix.
