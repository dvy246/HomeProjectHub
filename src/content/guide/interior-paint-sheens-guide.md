Choosing the right paint color is only half the battle. Selecting the correct paint sheen (or finish) is equally important, as it determines how durable, washable, and light-reflective your walls will be. 

## The Sheen Spectrum

Paint sheens range from completely flat (no shine) to high-gloss (mirror-like reflectivity). Higher sheen levels contain more binders/resins, making them harder and more resistant to moisture and scrubbing, but also more likely to highlight wall texture flaws.

---

## Sheen Comparison Table

| Sheen Level | Durability / Washability | Texture Hiding | Best Rooms & Surfaces |
|-------------|--------------------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| **Flat / Matte** | Low (hard to clean) | Excellent (hides bumps) | Ceilings, adult bedrooms, low-traffic areas |
| **Eggshell** | Moderate (wipeable) | Good (soft glow) | Living rooms, dining rooms, hallways |
| **Satin** | High (scrubable) | Moderate (slight sheen) | Family rooms, kids' rooms, foyers |
| **Semi-Gloss** | Very High (highly moisture-resistant) | Low (reflects defects) | Kitchens, bathrooms, baseboards, door trim |
| **High Gloss** | Extremely High (wear-resistant) | Very Low (reveals everything) | Cabinets, entry doors, furniture accents |

---

## Pro Selection Tips

1. **Ceilings**: Always use a true **flat paint** on ceilings. Light reflecting off a shiny ceiling will highlight seams, drywall nails, and texture discrepancies.
2. **Bathrooms & Kitchens**: High humidity and condensation can degrade low-sheen paints. Use **semi-gloss** or premium interior paints formulated specifically with mildewcides for high moisture.
3. **Trim, Baseboards, and Doors**: Baseboards take a beating from vacuum cleaners and shoes. Trim and doors are touched constantly. Paint these surfaces with a tough, enamel-based **semi-gloss** or **gloss** paint to allow for frequent scrubbing without wearing through the paint film.
