When pouring a patio, shed floor, or hot tub base, one of the most common questions is: **how many concrete bags for a 10x10 slab** do I need? 

The short answer depends on the depth (thickness) of your slab:
*   For a standard **4-inch deep 10x10 slab**, you will need **56 bags** of 80lb concrete mix.
*   For a heavy-duty **6-inch deep 10x10 slab**, you will need **84 bags** of 80lb concrete mix.

This guide breaks down the math, standard formulas, and bag yields so you can purchase the correct amount of material with zero waste.

---

## The Math: Volume Calculation Formula

To calculate the volume of a slab, use the standard geometric volume formula:

$$\text{Volume (Cubic Feet)} = \text{Length (ft)} \times \text{Width (ft)} \times \text{Thickness (ft)}$$

$$\text{Volume (Cubic Yards)} = \text{Volume (Cubic Feet)} \div 27$$

### Converting Inches to Feet
Thickness is usually measured in inches. Convert it to feet by dividing by 12:
*   **4 inches** = $4 \div 12 = 0.333\text{ ft}$
*   **6 inches** = $6 \div 12 = 0.500\text{ ft}$

## Concrete Bag Yields (80lb vs. 60lb)

Pre-mixed concrete bags do not cover square footage directly; they yield a specific volume of wet concrete.

*   An **80lb bag** yields approximately **$0.60\text{ cubic feet}$** of mixed concrete.
*   A **60lb bag** yields approximately **$0.45\text{ cubic feet}$** of mixed concrete.

### Bag Count Calculations for a 4-Inch 10x10 Slab ($33.3\text{ cu ft}$)
1.  **Using 80lb Bags:** $33.3\text{ cu ft} \div 0.60\text{ cu ft/bag} = 55.5$ bags. Round up to **56 bags**.
2.  **Using 60lb Bags:** $33.3\text{ cu ft} \div 0.45\text{ cu ft/bag} = 74.0$ bags. Round up to **74 bags**.

### Bag Count Calculations for a 6-Inch 10x10 Slab ($50.0\text{ cu ft}$)
1.  **Using 80lb Bags:** $50.0\text{ cu ft} \div 0.60\text{ cu ft/bag} = 83.3$ bags. Round up to **84 bags**.
2.  **Using 60lb Bags:** $50.0\text{ cu ft} \div 0.45\text{ cu ft/bag} = 111.1$ bags. Round up to **112 bags**.

## DIY Tips for Success

1.  **Subbase Preparation**: Excavate the area and compact a 2-to-4-inch gravel base. If the base is uneven, you will need more concrete than estimated.
2.  **Reinforcement**: Use rebar grids or welded wire mesh inside a 4-inch or 6-inch slab to prevent cracking and structural failure.
3.  **Mixing Strategy**: Mixing 60+ bags of concrete by hand in a wheelbarrow is exhausting. Consider renting a portable electric concrete mixer from a local hardware store.
