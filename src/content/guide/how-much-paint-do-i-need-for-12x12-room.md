A standard $12 \times 12\text{ foot}$ bedroom or office is one of the most common residential spaces homeowners choose to repaint. 

The short answer is: you will need **2 gallons of paint** for two coats on the walls of a standard $12 \times 12\text{ room}$ with $8\text{-foot}$ ceilings. If you are also painting the ceiling, purchase an additional **1 gallon** of specialized ceiling paint.

This guide explains how to calculate the paintable square footage of your walls and ceiling so you can get the perfect amount of paint.

---

## The Gallons-to-Square-Foot Coverage Rule

Before running calculations, know that a standard **gallon of paint covers between 350 and 400 square feet** of smooth wall surface. 
*   **1 Gallon** = 350 sq ft (used as a safe, practical estimate)
*   **2 Coats** = Doubling your square footage calculation

---

## Step-by-Step Wall Estimation for a 12x12 Room

Let's walk through the math for a room that is $12\text{ ft}$ long, $12\text{ ft}$ wide, and has $8\text{-foot}$ ceilings.

### Step 1: Calculate the Perimeter of the Room
Add all four wall lengths together:
$$\text{Perimeter} = 12\text{ ft} + 12\text{ ft} + 12\text{ ft} + 12\text{ ft} = 48\text{ ft}$$

### Step 2: Calculate Gross Wall Area
Multiply the perimeter by the wall height:
$$\text{Gross Area} = 48\text{ ft} \times 8\text{ ft} = 384\text{ sq ft}$$

### Step 3: Subtract Doors and Windows
You don't paint window panes or doors, so subtract them from the gross area.
*   **Standard Entry Door**: Subtract **$21\text{ sq ft}$**
*   **Standard Window**: Subtract **$15\text{ sq ft}$** (typically 3x5 ft)

Let's assume the room has 1 door and 1 window:
$$\text{Net Paintable Area} = 384\text{ sq ft} - 21\text{ sq ft} - 15\text{ sq ft} = 348\text{ sq ft}$$

### Step 4: Account for Multiple Coats
Most professional results require 2 coats of paint.
$$\text{Total Coverage Needed} = 348\text{ sq ft} \times 2 = 696\text{ sq ft}$$

### Step 5: Convert to Gallons
Divide the total coverage needed by 350 (average coverage per gallon):
$$\text{Gallons Required} = 696 \div 350 = 1.99\text{ gallons}$$
Round up to the nearest whole gallon: **2 gallons**.

## Ceiling Calculations

If you plan to paint the ceiling as well, calculate its square footage separately:
$$\text{Ceiling Area} = \text{Length} \times \text{Width}$$
$$\text{Ceiling Area} = 12\text{ ft} \times 12\text{ ft} = 144\text{ sq ft}$$

Since one gallon covers 350 sq ft, **1 gallon of ceiling paint** will easily cover the ceiling with two coats.
