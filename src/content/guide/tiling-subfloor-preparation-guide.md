Unlike carpet or vinyl, tile does not bend. If your subfloor flexes or shifts even slightly, the tiles will crack and the grout lines will crumble. Preparing a solid, deflection-free subfloor is the single most important part of any tile project.

## 1. Checking Deflection (Floor Flex)

Before laying tile, your floor joists must meet strict deflection limits:
* **Ceramic & Porcelain Tile**: Joist deflection must not exceed **L/360** (the span length divided by 360). For example, a 15-foot joist span can flex no more than 1/2 inch under design load.
* **Natural Stone**: Requires a stiffer floor meeting **L/720** deflection limits.

If your floor joists flex too much, you must reinforce them by sistering additional joists or adding support blocking underneath.

## Subfloor Requirements by Type

| Subfloor Material | Minimum Thickness | Required Underlayment |
|-------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Concrete Slab** | 4 inches | Self-leveler (if uneven) + Crack Isolation Membrane |
| **Plywood Subfloor** | 3/4 inch (tongue & groove) | Cement Backer Board (CBU) or Uncoupling Membrane |
| **OSB Subfloor** | 23/32 inch | Requires a secondary layer of 3/8" plywood overlay before CBU |

## 2. Plywood Underlayment Rules

* **Do not tile directly on OSB**: Standard thinset mortar does not bond well to the resins used in OSB.
* **Expansion Gaps**: Leave a 1/8-inch gap between plywood sheets to allow for wood expansion due to temperature and humidity fluctuations.
* **Fastener Pattern**: Secure the underlayment plywood sheets every 6 inches on the edges and every 8 inches in the field. Do not screw into the joists—only into the subfloor—to prevent nails from shearing when the floor settles.

## 3. Cement Backer Board (CBU) Installation

Cement board does not add structural strength; it provides a moisture-stable surface for thinset to bond with:
1. **Apply Thinset Below CBU**: Spread a layer of latex-modified thinset mortar over the plywood using a 1/4-inch square-notched trowel before laying the cement board.
2. **Screw Down CBU**: Fasten the backer board using corrosion-resistant screws spaced every 8 inches.
3. **Tape the Seams**: Fill all joints between cement boards with thinset and overlay with 2-inch alkali-resistant fiberglass mesh tape.
