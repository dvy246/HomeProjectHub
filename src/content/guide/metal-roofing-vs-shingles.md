Choosing between traditional asphalt shingles and modern metal roofing is one of the biggest decisions a homeowner will make. While asphalt shingles represent the majority of residential roof replacements, metal roofing is rapidly gaining market share due to its exceptional longevity.

## Key Comparison Metrics

| Metric | Asphalt Shingles | Metal Roofing (Standing Seam) |
|--------|------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Upfront Cost** | $3.50 – $6.50 per sq ft | $9.50 – $16.00 per sq ft |
| **Average Lifespan** | 15 – 25 years | 40 – 70 years |
| **Wind Resistance** | Up to 110-130 mph | Up to 140+ mph |
| **Fire Rating** | Class A (highest) | Class A (highest) |
| **Maintenance Need** | Moderate (algae, moss, wind damage) | Low (inspections, screw tightening) |
| **Energy Efficiency** | Low (absorbs heat) | High (reflects solar radiation) |

---

## 1. Upfront Installation Cost

Asphalt shingles are significantly cheaper to install. A typical 2,000 sq ft roof with asphalt shingles might cost **$7,000 to $13,000** fully installed. In contrast, a standing seam metal roof for the same home could cost **$19,000 to $32,000**. 

* **Why metal costs more**: Metal panels require highly skilled labor, specialized flashing details, and custom bending equipment.

## 2. Lifespan and Return on Investment (ROI)

* **Asphalt Shingles**: Will typically need to be replaced at least twice over a 50-year period. Heat exposure, freezing temperatures, and high winds wear down the asphalt granules.
* **Metal Roofing**: Often lasts the lifetime of the homeowner. Aluminum, steel, and copper are impervious to rot, pests, and moss growth, easily lasting 50+ years with minor maintenance.

## 3. Energy Efficiency and Cooling Costs

Metal roofing is a "cool roof" option. It naturally reflects solar heat back into the atmosphere rather than absorbing it into the attic, which can reduce summer cooling costs by up to **20% to 25%** compared to traditional dark asphalt shingle roofs.
