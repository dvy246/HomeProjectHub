Transition strips are the finishing details that bridge the gap between two different flooring surfaces. They ensure safety by eliminating trip hazards and protect the exposed edges of your flooring from chips and fraying.

## Types of Transition Moldings

Different floor heights and materials require specialized transition profiles:

1. **T-Molding**: A T-shaped strip used to bridge two floors of **equal height** (e.g. LVP to LVP, or laminate to tile). It requires a 1/2-inch gap between floors for expansion.
2. **Reducer**: Used to bridge floors of **different heights** (e.g. hardwood sloping down to vinyl).
3. **End Cap (Baby Threshold)**: Used when flooring terminates against a sliding glass door track, fireplace hearth, or high-pile carpet.
4. **Carpet Edge Grabber (Z-Bar)**: A metal strip that holds carpet tension while transitioning to hard floors.

## Choosing the Right Transition

| Floor A | Floor B | Recommended Transition | Why |
|---------|---------|------------------------|-----|
| LVP (5mm) | Tile (12mm) | Hard Surface Reducer | Bridges the 7mm height difference |
| Laminate | Laminate | T-Molding | Bridges the expansion gap at doorways |
| Hardwood | Carpet | Carpet Reder or End Cap | Caps the wood edge and locks down carpet |
| Flooring | Entry Door | End Cap / Threshold | Caps the raw flooring edge against the door jam |

## Installation Best Practices

* **Expansion Space**: Floating floors (laminate, LVP) expand and contract. Never nail or glue a transition strip directly to the floating floor boards—it must be anchored to the subfloor beneath using a track or glue, allowing the floor to slide underneath.
* **Securing to Concrete**: If installing over a concrete slab, you must drill holes with a masonry bit, insert plastic anchors, and screw the transition track down securely.
